Chapter 486: Roman -3-

Thump. Thump. Thump.

"Mom! Dad! I'm home!"

When Alice knocked on the wooden door and called out, a commotion arose inside the house.

From beyond the door, the voices and actions felt filled with anticipation and joy.

With a 'Clack!' sound, her mother, Jane, flung the door wide open.

Roman could tell whose genes Alice's skin and beauty strongly inherited.

"Alice!!! Welcome ho-me?"

Jane habitually lowered her head to look at Alice. Confirming someone else was there, she slowly raised her head and looked at Roman.

"Hello. My name is Roman."

"Oh, hello...?"

As Roman, holding a gift, bowed his head, his eye level roughly matched Jane's. Jane reflexively responded to the greeting, her long ears twitching, clearly flustered. Alice spoke with the brightest voice possible, along with Tero.

"Mom! This is the Platinum Adventurer, Roman-nim!"

Sssshhh!

Regardless of Tero scrambling onto Roman's head and perking up, Jane, having grasped the situation from her daughter's words, gaped and froze. Dorian, who had heard Alice from inside, came running out with a 'Clatter-bang!'

..

..

For the gift to bring during the visit, I tried hard to choose something not too expensive or burdensome with Alice. Their choice was a set of mug cups sold at a pottery workshop located in the capital. A typical household might find the price too high for everyday cups, but as a gift, it was an item with a price that could somehow be understood.

Seeing the mother and daughter organizing the cups in the kitchen, Roman shifted his gaze back. Roman, with his large frame, bowed his head while seated towards Dorian, who was fidgeting nervously.

"I apologize for dropping by so late. I thought Alice had mentioned it when she sent the letter."

Roman hadn't known that she hadn't said a word about bringing him along in the letter. Whether it was a surprise of sorts, if the goal was to surprise her parents, it was a perfectly successful one.

"Not at all...! It was actually us, my husband and I, who should have visited and paid our respects! We feel sorry that you had to come all the way to Edelman despite being the ones who should be expressing gratitude."

Dorian across from him shifted in his seat, muttering continuously in an awkward posture, unable to properly sit or stand.

"Please, feel free to speak comfortably."

"How could I possibly do that? I, Dorian, hope Roman-nim will treat me comfortably."

Dorian was acting so deferentially because he was truly realizing the presence of a Platinum. In reality, it was a situation more significant than if any high-ranking noble had barged into their home. Frankly, if told to leave the house, he'd have to leave without a word. That Roman, possessing such power, was meticulously using polite speech and showing such formality made Dorian feel breathless.

"I came here with my face hardened like iron because there's something I need to tell you."

For a Platinum to come all the way to the hick town of Edelman with something to say... At Roman's words, Dorian's Adam's apple bobbed as he swallowed nervously. Based on observation, the atmosphere wasn't bad, but a lingering sense of 'what if' took precedence.

"Did Alice perhaps make a mistake with Roman-nim...?"

"No. Alice is doing wonderfully. Not only with her adventurer duties, but she's also excellently performing her role as an assistant instructor at Lofti Academy, which has given me some breathing room."

"...Thank you. That's truly a relief."

Though I had heard it through letters, hearing unbelievable stories directly from Roman's mouth made it feel incredibly real. An instructor at Lofti Academy, even as an assistant, was a position unimaginable for anyone in this backwater town of Edelman. It was truly like a dragon emerging from a ditch.

'Alice...'

She was already navigating the world better than Dorian himself. Realizing his daughter's growth brought a lump to his throat, making his nose tingle.

"Roman-nim! Have some fruit!"

It was a timely salvation for Dorian. Alice, holding a plate of neatly sliced peaches, returned to the table, pulling Jane along.

"I cut them nicely, didn't I? I can cut fruit just as well as Rike now."

"That's right. Everyone has good dexterity."

Jane was about to apologize from behind for not having prepared much, but sensing something from Alice's face and actions as she chattered while clinging tightly to Roman, she shut her mouth.

"Thank you for the treat."

"Here! This is the guest fork!"

Alice naturally took the seat next to Roman and offered him a peach slice with the fork. Jane, sitting next to Dorian, stiffened with her back straight, sensing a strange atmosphere.

A household filled only with Alice's chatter. At this point, Roman started to feel awkward too.

'Hmm, this atmosphere...'

He wondered if it was okay to bring up their daughter's story with the couple frozen like stone. Experiencing Dorian's distant attitude made Roman become more cautious as well.

Mhuuuuu.

Roman, chewing a peach while pondering, raised his gaze.

'A spirit?'

Its appearance was so small, one might mistake it for a tiny cloud or a cotton ball. The spirit Jane controlled was about the size of a fist, floating lazily through the air. Roman's affinity with spirits, gained from defeating the wanderer Deganello, drew the spirit. It floated gently and settled on Roman's lap. If Tero, who was already occupying Roman's head, hadn't been there, it surely would have landed on his head.

"It's a spirit as cute as Tero."

Sssit!

"Y-You can really see spirits, Adventurer-nim...?"

This was something even Jane, who had been watching nervously, couldn't help but break her silence to ask. Of course, it's not impossible for others to see them, so she accepted that. But the fact that Tero, whom Alice controlled, and her own contracted spirit were approaching and following someone else was strange.

"Yes. However, I heard it's not good to touch another person's spirit. So when they approach, I just offer my body like this."

"Mom! I told you. It's real!"

Mhuuu!

Seeing the spirit lounging on Roman's thigh let out a pleased cry, Jane's eyes widened.

"A spirit approaching first, how is that possible...?"

Tero never approached Jane so affectionately, and even Anila, Jane's own spirit, despite spending a long time with Alice, had never shown such familiarity towards Roman. That was common sense regarding spirits. For Jane, whose profession was research, this situation sparked extreme curiosity.

"Jane."

"Umm... I apologize."

Dorian placed a hand on Jane's shoulder as her emotions began to heighten over her area of interest. Dorian was the only one present who couldn't see the spirit. As Jane became increasingly excited, Dorian, conversely, felt himself calming down and cautiously opened the conversation.

"We knew Alice was coming. I heard from a student in the afternoon class that there was a disturbance in the bustling district. They said magic was even used, so I thought I had to go check immediately."

"That was Olla and Bona first...!"

Seeing Alice raise her voice in reaction, Dorian raised his hand to calm his daughter.

"I don't intend to punish you for this. If Alice did it, there must have been a reason."

"Our daughter has a kind heart."

Dorian nodded at Jane's words and continued speaking to Roman.

"I thought if we went straight to the bustling district, we might miss each other. If it were Olla and Bona's parents, they'd likely storm our guild, so I decided to wait."

Alice pouted but looked anxiously at Dorian, wondering if something had happened. Roman also chimed in, with a hint of concern.

"If there were any damages, I will take responsibility one way or another. It was I who pushed Alice to handle the aftermath."

"Nothing happened. Since you didn't come, I just finished work and went home on time."

Alice breathed a sigh of relief upon hearing that nothing had happened.

"Besides, it wasn't just Olla and Bona there, but also Edelman's Roman... should I call him that? Anyway, that guy was there too. Since he knew who Roman-nim was, he must have told them to keep quiet."

"Hmm, that guy..."

Since there was someone with the same name in front of him, he was cautious about speaking ill of him carelessly.

'Even that damned guy has some sense when his life is on the line.'

Dorian had been prepared since hearing that Alice had taken action against Olla and Bona. Weren't their parents known as particularly overbearing figures even in Edelman? Yet, the day had been strangely quiet. The question surrounding that was now neatly resolved.

In fact, Dorian hadn't only heard that news today. He had also heard rumors from a disciple interested in outside affairs that were causing a stir in the capital. That the Empire's 5 Knights and the Church's Saint were in 'that kind of relationship' with the Platinum Adventurer. The name of the adventurer at the center of the topic was all too familiar to Dorian.

'The fact that he has time to come all the way here... is it just a baseless rumor?'

Dorian dared not ask. He wasn't even sure if he was in a position to exchange pleasantries with a Platinum, even if treated kindly. It could be a false rumor that would offend Roman right before him, or perhaps something true that he wouldn't want to mention.

'... '

Roman, on the opposite side, quietly watched the couple. Under the table, he could feel Alice holding his hand, playfully fiddling with her fingers, acting affectionately.

'I have to take the plunge.'

As Dorian and Jane remained silent with complicated expressions, Roman made a decision and decided to get to the point. If things continued like this, even if they waited until nightfall, he doubted they'd get a chance to speak naturally, let alone have dinner.

"As I mentioned earlier, the reason I came all this way to see you both is because there is something I need to say."

"We are listening intently..."

When Roman spoke without warning, the startled couple leaned forward, focusing intently. Taking a breath, Roman downed the cold water on the table in one gulp, then looked squarely at the couple and spoke, pouring as much emotion as possible into his voice to make it more persuasive.

"Alice and I are dating seriously with the intention of marriage. I apologize for not informing you of our relationship beforehand."

"...What?"

His mind couldn't process the information directly. Dorian and Jane blinked. Alice, with a huge smile on her face, cupped her flushed cheeks with both hands, squirming shyly.

"I have come here to ask for your permission to marry. Please grant us your blessing for Alice's marriage."

The atmosphere, which had started to loosen up, froze once again.
