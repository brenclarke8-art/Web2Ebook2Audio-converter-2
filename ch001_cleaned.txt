Chapter 487: Roman -4-

"I've come to receive your permission for marriage. Please grant me your blessing to marry Alice."

Unlike the couple who froze like stone at Roman's words, Alice reacted the most intensely, her face turning bright red.

'Kyaaa~!!'

Alice shrieked with joy, stomping her feet and exclaiming about how unbearable this ticklish feeling was.

Since the words were out, there was no need to hide anything anymore. The girl clung tightly to her lover, resting her shoulder against his and leaning her head on him.

Shaaaa!

Mwoohuuu.

The spirits indebted to Roman's body seemed to resonate, letting out sounds. To the ears of the girl in love, those cries sounded no different from a serenade.

"Heeheehee!"

Alice let out a playful, nasal giggle as she imagined a happy future with Roman, but then she sensed something amiss in the lingering silence and snapped her eyes open, looking ahead.

"Mom, Dad... why aren't you saying anything?"

"Alice. Your father and mother just need some time."

Even with Roman's gentle reassurance, Alice didn't relax the tension in her eyes. Nor did her talking stop. Roman, holding the prestigious Platinum rank, had come all the way to Edelman to formally ask for their permission. The situation where a clear answer wasn't immediately forthcoming was something Alice couldn't even imagine.

"Do you want me to live alone and be a lonely spinster?"

"...There's no way we'd want that."

Dorian finally spoke, which only made Alice's emotions surge further.

"Then do you want me to marry some coward who abandoned me in a dungeon, or some fool like Ullah's younger brother, Stranden?"

Jane and Dorian immediately denied it, shaking their heads. How could they possibly want that? For the couple, their daughter's happiness was always the top priority.

"Alice, that's enough."

Roman understood the reason for the silence from the two people opposite him. He stopped Alice, whose voice was about to rise in fury.

"R-Roman-nim... Still, this is really not okay!!"

Alice was agitated by the unexpected anxiety, wondering if she should oppose them if her parents didn't agree.

"It's alright. I'll explain slowly."

"..."

Her expression softened slightly when he firmly clasped her hand under the table. Roman, who had handed Alice a peach, spoke in a low voice within the home.

"I may not have the power to see the future or read minds, but I dare to answer that I know what you are worried about."

""...""

"I'll start with the honest truth. Though I am undeserving, I have a total of seven lovers, including Alice. Among them are figures renowned throughout the Empire."

This was the part that bothered Roman the most, given his values from his past life, but Dorian and Jane merely showed mild surprise.

"Even so, I promise that Alice will never be unjustly oppressed or discriminated against in her treatment simply because she is not a noble."

"!"

'I've hit the mark,' Roman thought, sensing their genuine surprise this time. Watching how this society functioned, he felt he had built up common sense, so he understood that for Dorian and Jane, this was difficult to accept.

'How much should I mention?'

He couldn't exactly prove that his mansion wasn't a place where women fought fiercely, creating blizzards of tension, or where schemes were hatched to seize the family succession. He had to convey, through his words, that Alice's current living situation was different from that of the nobles or wealthy individuals commonly known.

"It's completely different from the picture you're worried about, Mom and Dad. I'm not just looking to live a life as a broodmare or a living collection."

Appropriate supporting words. The direct terms Roman hesitated to use were spoken instead by Alice.

"Alice and the Saintess of the Order, Atra, are friends of the same age, and their relationship is by no means based on one-sided favors. As for the Dritna sisters-"

"Even if I marry Roman-nim, my training won't stop, and I won't become complacent. Just as Roman-nim said, while fulfilling my duties as a spouse, I will also pursue what I want-"

As the stories unfolded, the couple listened intently. And then, they were trapped in a cascade of past memories, recalling them. Had Alice ever shown interest in the opposite sex during her life? No, did she even have the leisure for such things in her life? The child who strove to overcome the low birthright bestowed by her flawed parents, running relentlessly towards the ceiling without a single complaint, always seemed precarious even as she grew. He worried she might break or snap if she only looked upwards, and he was even afraid that she might fall apart if she lost her driving force and stopped moving.

But.

'Has this time already come?'

Seeing his daughter introduce the partner of her life, he felt a strange emotion. He was keenly realizing that the day he had only imagined as a parent had arrived. Alice, who used to sleep all day in her cradle and toddle while holding onto her parents' legs, had grown up so much. Listening to the story, Alice's parents heard answers to their concerns, and they felt firsthand that Alice was living happily in a much better environment than they had imagined. Even if there were aspects they didn't fully understand, what else could parents do if not trust their child? They simply accepted it blindly and nodded.

"That is all I wished to convey."

"...Thank you."

When Roman finished his explanation, Dorian and Jane bowed their heads. Then, Jane turned to Alice and spoke.

"Alice..."

"Yes."

Their worries melted away, and the parents' true feelings began to surface. Just as Dorian supported Jane, and Jane supported Dorian, like two people who weren't blood-related but sustained each other. They felt relief, and guilt, that Alice, who had only run forward, had found someone to be her pillar and someone she could lean on.

"Mom was also new to being a mom... I'm sorry."

"..."

She should have smiled and congratulated her the moment she heard it. She realized her mistake and regretted it when she remained frozen, unable to speak. How anxious and sad must Alice have felt when the parents she trusted didn't respond? While feeling relieved that she had gained enoughä½è£ to look elsewhere, the worries that entered her mind made it difficult to speak easily.

"Sob..."

As Jane's eyes moistened and her voice trembled with tears, Dorian wiped his spouse's eyes. Alice buried her face in Roman's arm, hiding her expression. Dorian, who had comforted Jane, who was overcome with emotion and sniffling, looked up at Roman.

"...May I ask one thing?"

"Of course. I will answer with all sincerity."

Husband and wife are one body. While some couples might not be like that, Dorian and Jane were certain they were thinking the same thing right now.

"Roman-nim, why do you seek our couple's consent?"

After all, the other party was Roman, the pinnacle of adventurers, a Platinum. Even if he had impregnated Alice, who was so smitten, or informed them after the marriage was done, Dorian and Jane would have had no room to argue. So why? Why? For what reason? A simple couple with no titles and only their beautiful daughter as their pride, why did he bow his head and ask for their approval?

"It truly is a difficult world to make a living, isn't it?"

Roman comforted Alice, who was smearing tears and snot all over his clothes, and continued his next words in a gentle tone.

"If you ask anyone what a parent's duty is, they'll likely give a plausible answer about being responsible for and raising their children, but parents who truly overcome overwhelming hardships and fulfill that duty are rare in this world."

"..."

"Though it was the last time when I was young, I remember my parents' faces. My parents, on the verge of starvation, lost their reason and tried to trade me for the neighbor's child."

Dorian wasn't someone who wouldn't understand what those words implied. But perhaps Roman was using such parents as a negative example, or perhaps there was something hidden within his answer to his question. Dorian couldn't quite grasp Roman's true intentions.

"Do you resent them...?"

"I do not resent them."

The answer came out so clearly, as if there was no need to think about it. Dorian was speechless, but thinking that he wouldn't hear it if not now, he forced himself to speak.

"May I ask why?"

"I do not resent them because I saw my parents weeping then. Knowing how terrifying the fear of approaching starvation is, I have no intention of blaming their choice."

It wasn't that Roman's parents saw the anticipation of future fullness in their eyes at that moment. At that time, Roman's parents were crushed by guilt and sin, and their eyes were filled only with fear. Having laid the foundation, it was time to answer Dorian's question. Roman, uncharacteristically, avoided the gaze opposite him slightly, feeling the words were somewhat embarrassing.

"I know that you must have endured much hardship and toil after being given the title of parents."

"..."

"I wanted to express my gratitude for enduring those difficult days and raising Alice so uprightly."

Roman wanted Alice to maintain a harmonious relationship with her parents even after marriage. If he were to force things through power or authority, there was a possibility that relationship could falter, so he had to be cautious.

"I wish for you both to be present at the wedding ceremony. Please grant me permission for my marriage to Alice."

When Roman bowed his head respectfully once more after finishing his explanation, Dorian and Jane pushed their chairs back and stood up, bowing deeply to Roman.

"Please take good care of our daughter."
