Chapter 488: Roman -5-

As soon as permission was granted and the atmosphere lightened in a positive direction, the first ones to react were the spirits.

Tero, who had been itching to move while watching their reactions, went wild.

Shaat!

Moohoo!

Under the dining table, Tero and Anila. A lizard the size of a fist and a lamb tumbled around, tangled together.

I don't know what the spirits were doing, bickering amongst themselves, but they must surely be on good terms.

"When do you plan to return to the capital?"

"Alice wanted to have a reunion with you both... so we plan to depart leisurely tomorrow."

Dorian and Jane's gazes shifted towards their daughter.

They gazed at Alice, who had been hiding her face and crying softly until now.

As parents, they felt a sense of satisfaction, yet were swept up in an indescribable emotion. Alice probably couldn't clearly explain her own feelings either.

"And this is something I'd like you both to consider."

After receiving permission, they strove to understand each other.

They made an effort to share as many stories as possible.

There, Roman suggested to Alice's parents that they live in the capital.

It wasn't a sudden proposal out of the blue. He was only bringing it up after hearing honest opinions from everyone at the mansion.

There are other couples at the mansion, and while I understand the potential discomfort for everyone involved, finding a nice house in the capital is no trouble at all.

Dorian and Jane listened intently to Roman's proposal with surprised eyes.

When Roman finished speaking and quietly awaited their answer.

The couple exchanged a brief glance and Dorian spoke on their behalf.

"Thanks to our admirable daughter, we're hearing a proposal beyond our wildest dreams. Thank you so much. It feels like I'm dreaming just hearing it."

Just as Alice had anticipated, their thanks were followed by an extremely polite refusal.

There was absolutely no hint of hesitation or regret in Dorian's voice.

'They're good people.'

Even though Roman had said it as casually as possible, the burden of receiving accommodation in the capital for free must have been significant, but these two fundamentally possess a strong sense of moral responsibility.

Surely, for them, being able to see Alice, who is practically their everything, whenever they wished would be the greatest thing.

However, Dorian has disciples he is training, and Jane has research she is personally conducting.

Furthermore, they knew from past experience that constantly hovering near their daughter could unintentionally cause her concern.

Various reasons prevented them from accepting Roman's gracious offer.

In conclusion, Edelmann isn't so precious or wonderful that this couple couldn't leave it. However, they intend to fulfill the duties assigned to them.

For Dorian and Jane, Edelmann is a place where they lead a faithful daily life while waiting for their daughter to visit.

'...'

Roman could only admire them inwardly. It's truly difficult to meet people like this, whether in a past life or now.

Hearing this, I don't think it's foolish to refuse an opportunity for a life of luxury.

"When the time is right, I will propose it to you both again."

"We will consider it carefully as we go about our lives."

When Roman didn't push stubbornly and backed off easily.

Dorian showed a relaxed smile for the first time.

After that, Alice's parents did their best to get closer to Roman, just as much as he tried.

Jane prepared the late dinner she had been postponing, and Dorian engaged in light, feather-like conversation with Roman while waiting for their daughter to calm down.

...

Alice stopped crying and revealed her swollen eyes right when the meal was ready.

From Jane's perspective, it was fortunate that she had stocked up on ingredients after receiving Alice's letter.

"I wonder if it will suit your taste."

"It's deadly delicious. My palate is quite similar to Alice's."

The thoughtfulness of parents waiting for their daughter. At a glance, the table was filled only with dishes Alice loved.

"...Mom. Dad."

"What is it?"

Alice, who had been sniffling while putting food into her mouth, finally spoke.

The girl's voice, subtly trembling, still held remnants of her emotions.

"Can we all come visit together next time? I want to show you what kind of wonderful people I'm living with."

Alice's voice, saying she would bring the Saint of the Order and the Empire's 5 Knights.

Dorian and Jane, despite imagining a scenario so overwhelming that it choked them and made food impossible to swallow.

reflexively nodded at their daughter's teary eyes, signaling their consent.

··

··

"I was thinking of getting a room for you in the bustling part of Edelmann and sending you there."

Finishing the meal, Roman stood up, looking at the pitch-darkness outside the window. He said this intending for the family to spend their long-awaited time together.

Even though this had been discussed beforehand, Alice pouted, displeased, and expressed her dissatisfaction. However, she couldn't object because she had previously agreed, considering her parents' position.

"How could we possibly send our guest and future son-in-law out at this late hour?"

Since Alice didn't stop him, Dorian caught Roman.

Roman and Alice's lips twitched at the word 'son-in-law'.

"I'm really fine."

"I know you're saying that out of consideration for us. However, if you, Roman-nim, who went to the trouble of visiting us, were to stay at an inn, it would put us in a more difficult position."

"..."

"Besides, rumors spread incredibly fast in Edelmann. If word gets out that we made the son-in-law from the capital sleep outside, we wouldn't be able to show our faces in public."

They mentioned having spare blankets and rooms, seeming ready to offer their own bed to Roman. Roman, finding that prospect too awkward to bear, politely declined, and the girl intervened.

"Roman-nim should naturally sleep in my room with me!"

Alice's bright yellow eyes flashed, seizing the opportunity. Before Dorian could say anything, Alice grabbed Roman's sleeve and pulled him along.

Roman followed his lover, who was leading him to her room.

Click! The door connecting to the living room closed.

"Hooo..."

"So this is the room Alice used to stay in."

Alice's room had a small bed. It was barely large enough for Roman's feet to stick out if he lay down. Although the room had been unused for quite some time, Jane managed it daily so Alice could stay comfortably whenever she returned, ensuring no dust accumulated. By the window, there was a pot filled with moist soil, perhaps for Tero.

"I-it's kind of embarrassing..."

Alice's expression softened, drenched in affection. Looking back, the girl placed her hand over her chest, her heart pounding uncontrollably. Alice, her eyes still swollen from silent, great sobbing, kept realizing the situation as she looked at her lover.

"Are you starting to feel it now?"

Held in Roman's arms, Alice shyly nodded. The giddy feeling of being engaged, having received permission from her parents. She could hardly stay still.

'My husband is so cool...'

Shyness kept the word 'husband' from escaping her lips; she only called him that in her heart. Her own parents seemed genuinely moved in an instant. Alice herself had heard it clearly with her own ears - his eloquent words, filled with earnest sincerity, were so precious that they couldn't possibly refuse.

"R-Roman-nim..."

"Yes?"

"I've fallen for you all over again, Roman-nim..."

"It's my honor."

His words from when they arrived were echoed back. As Roman smiled and affectionately squeezed Alice's cheeks.

Alice stood on her tiptoes, squeezed her eyes shut, and pouted her lips like a duck.

"K-kiss... please!"

She couldn't handle any more intense physical intimacy right now. She definitely couldn't bear it.

Smack! The sound echoed as a satisfying sensation lingered on her lips. Alice cupped her burning cheeks with both hands.

'...My heart feels like it's going to explode.'

The girl's heart pounded violently, threatening to burst, and her face burned with fever. If Jane hadn't knocked on the door, announcing she'd brought extra blankets, who knows what might have happened driven by instinct afterward.

····

Although it was a room for just the two of them, the bed was too small to share. But Alice wasn't about to give up; Roman laid the blanket he received from Jane on the floor, and Alice, who had been on the bed, quietly got down and snuggled close. No matter what, he had no intention of making love in this cramped, humble home. If he used mana to block the sounds, it would be like advertising to Jane, a mage, that he was with her daughter. That wouldn't do. He had to maintain the image he'd built up, didn't he?

Despite that, the night was incredibly enjoyable rather than disappointing. Alice, snuggled under the blanket, discussed future plans and anticipated joys. As they exchanged stories of their past, like their first meeting at the Adventurer's Guild or their chance encounter in a dungeon, sunlight began to stream through the window. As the sun rose, the movements of the couple preparing for work could be felt beyond the door.

Roman and Alice decided to return to the capital that afternoon. It was after they had lunch together at Dorian and Jane's martial arts guild, who had made time for them.

"Well then, we shall take our leave. We will visit again soon with good news."

"We will be waiting. Alice, stay healthy and write to us."

"Okay! Mom and Dad, you stay healthy too!"

Before leaving, Roman stopped by the Edelmann Adventurer's Guild and made sure to greet the person in charge, leaving his mark. Having done some basic preventative work, he finally felt at ease and could depart.

'I guess I passed the test as a son-in-law.'

The visit to Edelmann, which was more eventful than expected, concluded with satisfactory results. The two boarded the gate with light hearts.

And so.

As they neared the mansion in the capital, with Alice chattering away, filling his ears with pleasantries. Roman sensed Gleipnir and Nachal, who seemed to have woken up fully rested from their slumber, making their presence known. At the same time, he began to worry about how he would explain these two to everyone.
